About This Monitor

What This Is

Qualcomm News Monitor is a daily briefing tool that aggregates Qualcomm-related coverage from 20+ foreign media sources across six monitoring dimensions. It updates automatically at 07:00 CST (UTC+8) every day, providing a synthesized overview of the most important developments.

Methodology

Articles are sourced from two channels:

Native RSS feeds — Sources like TechCrunch, The Verge, VentureBeat, EE Times, IPWatchdog, and others publish full RSS feeds that include titles, descriptions, and links. These are fetched directly using standard RSS parsing.

Google News RSS aggregation — Premium sources like Reuters, Bloomberg, Wall Street Journal, Financial Times, and CNBC do not offer public full-text RSS feeds. For these sources, we use Google News's publicly available RSS search to surface headlines and links that mention Qualcomm. This is a legal, public-facing aggregation method that respects paywalls and only surfaces publicly indexed content.

All articles are then filtered for Qualcomm relevance using keyword matching, tagged by section and sub-category, deduplicated, and summarized. We never scrape full article text — summaries are derived solely from RSS-provided descriptions or headline-derived teasers. Every article links back to its original source.

Monitoring Dimensions

A. Core Businesses — Semiconductors, wireless communication, mobile chips, PC chips & computing platforms, automotive chips & platforms, IoT & XR.

B. IP & Legal — Standard-essential patents (SEP), intellectual property, patent litigation, FRAND and licensing disputes, regulatory and antitrust actions.

C. Growth Areas — On-device AI and edge AI, AI PC, embodied AI and robotics, in-vehicle infotainment and ADAS, XR and spatial computing.

D. Macro Environment — Customers and partners, supply chain dynamics, geopolitics and export controls.

E. Competitors — Apple, MediaTek, Intel, NVIDIA, AMD, Samsung, Broadcom, Huawei and other relevant competitors.

F. Key Stakeholders — Standards bodies (3GPP, ETSI, IEEE), industry alliances (Wi-Fi Alliance, Bluetooth SIG, USB-IF, O-RAN), regulators, OEMs, foundries, and platform partners.

Limitations

Paywalled sources: Bloomberg, WSJ, FT, and The Information operate behind paywalls. Their articles appear as headlines with links; full text is not available through public RSS. Law360 IP is entirely subscriber-only; only Google News headlines are surfaced.

Timeliness: RSS feeds are fetched once daily at 23:00 UTC. Breaking news after this window will appear in the next day's briefing.

Coverage gaps: Articles may be missed if they do not appear in the monitored RSS feeds or Google News results at fetch time. The keyword-based relevance filter may occasionally misclassify articles.

No full-text scraping: To respect publishers' terms of service, we do not scrape article bodies. Summaries are limited to the information available in RSS feed descriptions.

Update Schedule

The build pipeline runs daily at 23:00 UTC, which corresponds to 07:00 China Standard Time (UTC+8). The site is automatically redeployed with fresh data immediately after each build.
